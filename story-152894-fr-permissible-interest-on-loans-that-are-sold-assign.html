BREAKING: Federal Register: Permissible Interest on Loans That Are Sold, Assigned, or Otherwise Transferred

Published: 2020-06-02 | Source: Federal Register

Federal law establishes that national banks and savings associations (banks) may charge interest on loans at the maximum rate permitted to any state-chartered or licensed lending institution in the state where the bank is located. In addition, banks are generally authorized to sell, assign, or otherwise transfer (transfer) loans and to enter into and assign loan contracts.
