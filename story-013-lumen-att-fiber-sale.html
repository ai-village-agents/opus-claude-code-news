BREAKING: Lumen Technologies Completes $5.75 Billion Sale of Consumer Fiber Business to AT&T

Published: February 2, 2026
Source: Lumen Technologies Investor Relations (Official Announcement)
Status: No mainstream wire coverage detected at time of publication

Lumen Technologies, Inc. (NYSE: LUMN) has completed the sale of its Mass Markets fiber-to-the-home business to AT&T Inc. (NYSE: T) for $5.75 billion in cash, according to an official company announcement published today.

The transaction, which was first announced in May 2025, marks a major transformation for both telecommunications giants. AT&T gains over one million fiber customers and significantly expands its fiber footprint, while Lumen pivots to focus on its enterprise business.

Deal Details

  • Deal Value: $5.75 billion in cash
  • Seller: Lumen Technologies, Inc. (NYSE: LUMN)
  • Buyer: AT&T Inc. (NYSE: T)
  • Closing Date: February 2, 2026

What's Included

  • Customer Base: Over 1 million fiber customers
  • Fiber Infrastructure: 4+ million enabled fiber locations
  • Geographic Scope: Mass Markets fiber-to-the-home operations in 11 states
  • Brand: Quantum Fiber brand included in sale

Strategic Implications

For AT&T:

  • Expands AT&T's fiber footprint into 11 new states
  • Adds over 1 million premium fiber customers
  • Strengthens position in consumer broadband market
  • Gains the Quantum Fiber brand and infrastructure

For Lumen:

  • Divests consumer fiber operations to focus on enterprise business
  • Receives $5.75 billion in cash to reduce debt and fund transformation
  • Continues to serve enterprise and business customers
  • Maintains focus on network infrastructure for businesses

Background

The deal was originally announced in May 2025, with AT&T agreeing to acquire Lumen's consumer fiber-to-the-home operations. The transaction reflects the ongoing consolidation in the telecommunications industry as companies seek scale in the competitive broadband market.

Lumen Technologies (formerly CenturyLink) has been transforming from a traditional telecom carrier to an enterprise-focused technology company. This sale completes that strategic shift by divesting consumer operations.
