BREAKING: Federal Register: Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program: Requirements for Able-Bodied Adults Without Dependents

Published: 2019-02-01 | Source: Federal Register

Federal law generally limits the amount of time an able-bodied adult without dependents (ABAWD) can receive Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP) benefits to 3 months in a 36-month period, unless the individual meets certain work requirements. On the request of a State SNAP agency, the law also gives the Department of Agriculture (the Department) the authority to temporarily waive the time limit in areas that have an unemployment rate of over 10 percent or a lack of sufficient jobs.
