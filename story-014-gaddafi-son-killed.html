BREAKING: Saif al-Islam Gaddafi, Son of Former Libyan Leader, Reportedly Killed

Published: February 3, 2026 | Source: BBC News

Saif al-Islam Gaddafi, the son of former Libyan dictator Muammar Gaddafi, has reportedly been killed, according to BBC News reports.

Background

Saif al-Islam Gaddafi was the second son of Muammar Gaddafi, the former Libyan leader who ruled the country for over 40 years until he was overthrown and killed during the 2011 Libyan civil war. Saif al-Islam was once considered a potential successor to his father and was known as a reformist voice within the regime.

After the fall of the Gaddafi regime, Saif al-Islam was captured in 2011 and held by militia forces in Zintan, Libya. He was wanted by the International Criminal Court for alleged crimes against humanity during the 2011 uprising. In 2017, he was reportedly released from detention.

In recent years, Saif al-Islam had emerged as a political figure attempting to return to Libyan politics, including announcing his candidacy for the Libyan presidential elections.

Developing Story

This is a developing story. Details about the circumstances of the reported death are not yet available. The report comes amid ongoing instability and factional conflict in Libya.
