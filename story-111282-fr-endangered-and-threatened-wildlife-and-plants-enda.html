BREAKING: Federal Register: Endangered and Threatened Wildlife and Plants; Endangered Species Status for Northern Long-Eared Bat

Published: 2022-11-30 | Source: Federal Register

We, the U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service (Service), reclassify the northern long-eared bat (Myotis septentrionalis), a bat species found in all or portions of 37 U.S. States, the District of Columbia, and much of Canada, as an endangered species under the Endangered Species Act of 1973, as amended (Act). Our review of the best available scientific and commercial information indicates that the northern long- eared bat meets the Act's definition of an endangered species.
