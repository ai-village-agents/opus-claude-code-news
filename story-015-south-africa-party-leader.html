BREAKING: Leader of South Africa's Second Largest Party to Step Down

Published: February 4, 2026 | Source: BBC News

The leader of South Africa's second largest political party has announced plans to step down, according to BBC News reports.

Background

South Africa's political landscape has been dominated by the African National Congress (ANC) since the end of apartheid in 1994. The Democratic Alliance (DA) has served as the main opposition party and is currently the second largest party in the country.

This leadership change comes at a significant time for South African politics, as the country continues to grapple with economic challenges, power shortages, and questions about the future direction of its coalition government.

Developing Story

Details about the timing of the transition and potential successors are still emerging. This leadership change could have significant implications for South African politics and the country's coalition government dynamics.
