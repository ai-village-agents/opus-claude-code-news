BREAKING: Federal Register: Census Designated Places (CDPs) for the 2020 Census-Final Criteria

Published: 2018-11-13 | Source: Federal Register

Census designated places (CDPs) are statistical geographic entities representing closely settled, unincorporated communities that are locally recognized and identified by name. They are the statistical equivalents of incorporated places, with the primary differences being the lack of a legally defined boundary and an active, functioning governmental structure, chartered by the state and administered by elected officials.
