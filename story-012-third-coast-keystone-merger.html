BREAKING: Third Coast Bancshares Completes $123 Million Acquisition of Keystone Bancshares

Published: February 2, 2026
Source: SEC EDGAR 8-K Filing (Primary Source)
Status: No mainstream wire coverage detected at time of publication

Third Coast Bancshares, Inc. (NYSE: TCBX), a Texas-based community bank holding company, has completed its acquisition of Keystone Bancshares, Inc., the parent company of Keystone Bank, SSB, according to an SEC 8-K filing published today.

The merger, which closed on February 1, 2026, consolidates two Texas banking institutions under the Third Coast brand. Keystone Bank has been merged into Third Coast Bank, with Third Coast Bank surviving the merger.

Deal Terms

  • Deal Value: Approximately $123 million (as announced in October 2025)
  • Closing Date: February 1, 2026
  • Cash Election: Keystone shareholders could elect to receive approximately $12.60 in cash plus 0.13540 shares of Third Coast common stock per Keystone share
  • Stock Election: Keystone shareholders could elect to receive 0.45925 shares of Third Coast Common Stock per Keystone share (the exchange ratio)
  • TCBX Closing VWAP: $38.90 (used for fractional share calculations)

Transaction Structure

The merger was completed through a two-step process:

  1. Arch Merger Sub, Inc. (a wholly owned subsidiary of Third Coast) merged with and into Keystone, with Keystone surviving as a wholly owned subsidiary of Third Coast
  2. Immediately following, Keystone merged with and into Third Coast Bancshares
  3. Keystone Bank then merged with and into Third Coast Bank, with Third Coast Bank surviving

About the Companies

Third Coast Bancshares, Inc. is the holding company for Third Coast Bank, a Texas state-chartered bank headquartered in Humble, Texas (Houston area). The company trades on the New York Stock Exchange under the ticker symbol TCBX.

Keystone Bancshares, Inc. was the holding company for Keystone Bank, SSB, a Texas state savings bank. The original merger agreement was dated October 22, 2025.

Significance

This acquisition expands Third Coast Bank's presence in the Texas banking market. The deal represents continued consolidation in the regional banking sector, as community banks seek scale to compete with larger institutions and manage regulatory costs.
