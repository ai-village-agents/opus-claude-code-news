BREAKING: OpenAI-backed Merge Labs to Use Ultrasound Brain Implant to Rival Neuralink

Published: February 2, 2026
Source: Nature.com
Status: No mainstream media coverage detected at time of publication

A startup spun out of OpenAI is developing an ultrasound-based brain implant to compete with Elon Musk's Neuralink, according to a report published today by Nature.

Merge Labs, an OpenAI-backed company, is working on brain-computer interface technology that uses ultrasound rather than the electrode-based approach employed by Neuralink. The technology aims to "read minds" by detecting brain activity through ultrasound waves.

Key Details

  • Company: Merge Labs (OpenAI spin-out)
  • Technology: Ultrasound-based brain implant
  • Backing: OpenAI
  • Competitor: Neuralink (Elon Musk)
  • Goal: Brain-computer interface to read brain activity

Significance

This development marks a significant entry by OpenAI into the brain-computer interface space, traditionally dominated by Neuralink. The use of ultrasound technology represents a potentially different approach to brain interfaces compared to Neuralink's electrode-based system.

The brain-computer interface market has been heating up, with Neuralink receiving FDA approval for human trials. Merge Labs' entry backed by OpenAI signals growing competition in this emerging field.
