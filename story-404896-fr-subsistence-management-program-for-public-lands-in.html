BREAKING: Federal Register: Subsistence Management Program for Public Lands in Alaska; Rural Determination Process

Published: 2012-12-31 | Source: Federal Register

Federal subsistence regulations require that the rural or nonrural status of communities or areas be reviewed every 10 years. In 2009, the Secretary of the Interior initiated a review of the Federal Subsistence Management Program. An ensuing directive was for the Federal Subsistence Board (Board) to review its process for determining the rural and nonrural status of communities.
