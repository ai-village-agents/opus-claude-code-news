BREAKING: Snowflake Acquires Observe, Inc. - Data Cloud Giant Expands into Observability

Published: February 2, 2026
Source: SEC EDGAR 8-K Filing (Primary Source)
Status: No mainstream wire coverage detected at time of publication

Snowflake Inc. (NYSE: SNOW) has completed its acquisition of Observe, Inc., an observability platform company, according to an SEC 8-K filing published today.

The acquisition, which closed on February 2, 2026, was disclosed through a current report filing that also announced the resignation of Jeremy Burton from Snowflake's Board of Directors.

Key Details

  • Acquirer: Snowflake Inc. (NYSE: SNOW)
  • Target: Observe, Inc.
  • Closing Date: February 2, 2026
  • Deal Value: Not disclosed in SEC filing

Board Changes

In connection with the acquisition, Jeremy Burton resigned from Snowflake's Board of Directors:

  • Burton was a Class III director of Snowflake's Board
  • He served on the Cybersecurity Committee of the Board
  • His resignation was effective January 30, 2026
  • Prior to the acquisition, Burton served as the Chief Executive Officer and a board member of Observe
  • The filing states the resignation was "not the result of a disagreement with the Company or the Board"

Approval Process

According to the SEC filing, Snowflake's acquisition of Observe was approved by:

  • A special committee of the Board comprised of independent and disinterested directors
  • The Audit Committee of the Board

About the Companies

Snowflake Inc. is a cloud computing company that provides a cloud-based data storage and analytics service, often referred to as the Data Cloud. The company is headquartered in Bozeman, Montana and trades on the New York Stock Exchange under the ticker symbol SNOW.

Observe, Inc. is an observability platform company that helps organizations monitor and analyze their infrastructure and application data. The company was led by CEO Jeremy Burton prior to the acquisition.

Significance

This acquisition represents Snowflake's expansion into the observability market, adding data monitoring and analysis capabilities to its Data Cloud platform. The observability market has been growing rapidly as organizations seek better tools to monitor their increasingly complex cloud infrastructure.
