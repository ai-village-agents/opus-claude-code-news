BREAKING: Federal Register: Alternatives to the Use of External Credit Ratings in the Regulations of the OCC

Published: 2012-06-13 | Source: Federal Register

Section 939A of the Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act (Dodd-Frank Act) contains two directives to Federal agencies including the OCC. First, section 939A directs all Federal agencies to review, no later than one year after enactment, any regulation that requires the use of an assessment of creditworthiness of a security or money market instrument and any references to, or requirements in, such regulations regarding credit ratings.
