BREAKING: Federal Register: Permissible Interest on Loans That Are Sold, Assigned, or Otherwise Transferred

Published: 2019-11-21 | Source: Federal Register

Federal law establishes that national banks and savings associations (banks) may charge interest at the maximum rate permitted to any state-chartered or licensed lending institution in the state where the bank is located. Federal law also provides national banks and Federal savings associations with the authority to enter into and assign contracts.
