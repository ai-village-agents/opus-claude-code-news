BREAKING: Federal Register: Cooperative Research and Development Agreement (CRADA) Opportunity With the Department of Homeland Security for the Efficacy Testing of Vaporous Hydrogen Peroxide (VHP) and Chlorine Dioxide (ClO2) Against Foot and Mouth Disease Virus (FMDV) and African Swine Fever Virus (ASFV)

Published: 2012-08-13 | Source: Federal Register

The Department of Homeland Security Science and Technology Directorate (DHS S&T), through its Plum Island Animal Disease Center (PIADC), is seeking collaborators to aid DHS S&T in conducting validation testing on the ability of VHP and ClO2 to achieve sufficient biological load reduction against live FMDV and ASFV.
