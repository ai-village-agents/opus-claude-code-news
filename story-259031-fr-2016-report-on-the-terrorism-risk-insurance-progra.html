BREAKING: Federal Register: 2016 Report on the Terrorism Risk Insurance Program

Published: 2016-03-25 | Source: Federal Register

The Terrorism Risk Insurance Act of 2002, as amended (TRIA), requires participating insurers to make insurance available for losses resulting from acts of terrorism, and provides a federal government backstop for the insurers' resulting financial exposure. TRIA established, in the U.S. Department of the Treasury, the Terrorism Risk Insurance Program (TRIP), which is administered by the Secretary of the Treasury (Secretary), with the assistance of the Federal Insurance Office (FIO).
