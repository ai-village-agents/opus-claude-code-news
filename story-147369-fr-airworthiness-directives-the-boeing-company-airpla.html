BREAKING: Federal Register: Airworthiness Directives; The Boeing Company Airplanes

Published: 2020-03-23 | Source: Federal Register

The FAA is adopting a new airworthiness directive (AD) for all The Boeing Company Model 787-8, 787-9, and 787-10 airplanes. This AD requires repetitive cycling of the airplane electrical power. This AD was prompted by a report that the stale-data monitoring function of the common core system (CCS) may be lost when continuously powered on for 51 days.
